This page includes what you will need to know in order to read Faulkner's magnum opus, The Sound and the Fury.
One of the most abandoned pieces of classic literature, and it is easy to see why. While reading this novel you will
encounter some of the most wack stream-of-consciousness writing out there, with Faulkner jumping from
past to present to future like a [literal] idiot. However, if you complete it, you will be rewarded.

The novel takes place in the fictional Yoknapatawpha County in Mississippi, one that you will have to be familiar with if you decide to study Faulkner.
The very picture of the Southern Gothic, the fictional county mirrors the decline of the Compsons
as well as the broader decay of the Old South

The Compsons:
The Compsons are a crumbling southern dynasty, a once slave-owning family struggling to maintain honor in the waning hours of its existence.
